kawaba

the language of parts

compound words

vocabulary

    mi - 1st person pronoun, I, me we, us, my, our
    to - 2nd person pronoun, you, you (plural), your
    la - 3rd person pronoun, he, she, it, they, his, her, their
    sa - person, someone, entity, human
    si - this, that, these, those
    ka - kind, variety, sort, type
    wa - say, talk, speak, communication
    ba - part, piece, section, component
    ku - time, event, period, duration
    ma - place, earth, land, soil, world

roots

a word in kawaba is made up of one or more roots. the first root in a word is the head root and carries the base meaning of the word. this can be modified by subsequent modifier roots.

    In the word {¤|language} {ka|kind, variety} {wa|say, speak}, ka is the head root and wa is its modifier root. This forms the compound word meaning language, a kind of speaking.
    This compound word can then be modified by the root ba to form the name of the language {¤|kawaba} {ka|kind, variety} {wa|say, speak} {ba|part, piece}, the language of parts.

compound modifiers

a compound word can itself be used as a modifier by placing a hyphen before the compound modifier in the word. This is pronounced as stress on the following syllable. For example, a linguist would be {¤ |linguist} {sa|person, someone} {-|compound modifier} {ka|kind, variety} {wa|say, speak}. It is generally advisable to limit the number of compound modifiers used in a word as using multiple creates ambiguity as to what is being modified.
