an ìnglish speling réform!

it's no secret that english spelling kinda sucks. being the love child of germanic, latin, french, and a bit of greek, english spelling is a mess of inconsistent rules and exceptions. this reform is my attempt at the design challenge of simplifying english spelling, specifically to be entirely consistent and pleasing to me personally.

kònsonants

labial dental alveolar post-alveolar palatal velar glottal
m n ng /ŋ/
p t ch /tʃ/ k
b d j /dʒ/ g
f th /θ/ s sh /ʃ/ h
v th /ð/ z zh /ʒ/
l r y w

perfectly consistent! every distinct speech sound has one unique representation, with the exception of the dental frictives, which are both represented by 'th' for aesthetic reasons. also for aesthetic reasons, the historic voiceless labial-velar approximant is spelled 'wh'.

each word part, or morpheme, is always spelled one way, regardless of the final word's pronunciation. this means that the english suffixes -s/-es and -'s are always spelled with an 's', even when pronounced as /z/ and the suffix -ed is always spelled with a 'd', even when pronounced as /t/.

vowels

short j-diphthongs w-diphthongs historic r-vowels
ì /ɪ/ é /ɪj/ ir /ɪː(r)/
è /ɛ/ á /ɛj/ er /ɛː(r)/
à /a/ í /ɑj/ ou /aw/ ar /ɑː/
ò /ɔ/ oi /oj/ ó /əw/ or /oː(r)/
ü /ɵ/ ú /ʉw/ ür /ɵː(r)/
any /ə/ ur /əː(r)/
u /ʌ/

this vowel system is based on geoff lindsay's analysis of recieved pronounciation and historic english short and long vowels. blame the great vowel shift for it being a mess. there are some extra rules, again for aesthetic reasons:

any unmarked vowel is pronounced as the reduced vowel /ə/ unless it is the only vowel in the morpheme, in which case it can be pronounced as the corresponding short vowel.

when the digraph diphthongs 'oi' and 'ou' occur before a vowel or morpheme finally, they are spelled 'oy' and 'ow' respectively.

when the sounds /ɑː/ and /oː/ occur without the presence of a historic r, they are spelled 'ā' and 'ō' respectively.

the letter 'y' at the end of the word represents the sound /ɑj/ if monosyllablic, or /ɪj/ if polysyllablic.

the north wind and the sun

the north wind and the sun wur dispyúting which woz the strongur when a tràvelur kám alòng rapd in a worm klók. thá agréd that the wun hú furst sukséded in máking the tràvelur ták hiz klók of shüd be konsìdurd strongur than the uthur. then the north wind blú az hard az he küd, but the mor hé blú the mor klósly did the tràvelur fóld hiz klók around him; and at last the north wind gáv up the atempt. then the sun shínd out wormly, and imídíatly the tràvelur tük of hiz klók. and so the north wind woz oblíjd to konfès that the sun woz the strongur of the tú.
