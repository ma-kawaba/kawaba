buddhism explained in toki pona!

toki pona is a minimalist constructed language created by sonja lang in 2001. it was designed to simplify thoughts and promote positive thinking. the language has only 137 essential root words and a simple grammar, making it one of the smallest languages in the world. here are the principles of buddhism explained in toki pona.

nasin sewi Puta

nasin sewi Puta (toki Pali: buddha, 'lape ala') li nasin sewi Palata tan jan Sitata Kotama (toki Pali: Siddhattha Gotama) anu jan Puta. tenpo pini la jan Puta li pana e sona pi pilin ike e nasin meso.

jan Puta li pana e sona ni:

  • pilin Tuka (toki Pali: dukkha, 'ante') - ijo ale li ante lon tenpo ale. jan ale li ken pilin ike tan ni. nimi ni li pilin Tuka.

  • kama Samutaja (toki Pali: samudaya, 'kama') - wile ike pi pilin pona li kama tan pilin ike. nimi ni li wile Tana (toki Pali: taṇhā, 'wile telo'). taso, jan ale li pilin ike kin tan ni: ona li ken ala kama jo e ijo ale wile. nimi ni li sike Sansala (toki Pali: saṃsāra, 'tawa sike pi nasin ala')

  • pini Nilota (toki Pali: nirodha, 'pini') - jan li pini e wile ike ni la ona li ken pini e pilin ike wile e sike Sansala. nimi ni li tenpo Nipana (toki Pali: nibbāna, 'seli ala')

  • nasin Maka (toki Pali: magga, 'nasin') - jan li ken pini e wile ike ni kepeken nasin Puta. nimi ni li nasin Maka.
